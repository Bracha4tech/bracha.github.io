Project 03 · Engineering Prototype · Grand Challenges LLC · Team L15

BerrySnug

Fall 2025 to Spring 2026 · Passive Freeze Protection for Small Georgia Farms

The Problem

Georgia berry and specialty crop farmers regularly lose entire harvests to brief radiation frost events, nights when temperatures drop below critical damage thresholds for blueberries (27°F), strawberries (30°F), and peaches. Existing solutions like overhead sprinklers, wind machines, and row covers alone are costly, labor-intensive, or insufficiently protective for small farms.

The Solution

BerrySnug pairs a passive solar thermal battery (a dark, insulated 55-gallon water drum) with a sealed row cover tent to create a warmer microclimate with zero energy input. The drum absorbs solar heat during the day and releases it overnight inside the enclosed tent, keeping plant-level air temperature 2 to 4 degrees Fahrenheit above outside ambient during a marginal freeze night.

My Role

As the Computer Engineering member of Team L15, I led data logging architecture (Govee/Inkbird sensor setup, CSV export, automated ΔT calculation) and co-authored the full 12-section prototype specification document. Contributed to sensor placement planning, test protocol design, and the 6-week build-and-test timeline. Collaborated with Maya (Mechanical), Emily (Business), and Palak (Industrial) across the full design cycle.

Key Stakeholder

Beth McQuaig-McIntyre of Anna's Garden, a 54-acre blueberry farm in Wilcox County, Georgia. Design decisions were grounded in her operational reality, not abstract specs.
