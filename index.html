About Me

Engineer by training.
Community-builder by choice.

My engineering journey began not in a formal lab, but in my community in Yaoundé, Cameroon — where observing systemic challenges in waste management and resource access led me to co-found BrachSpace, my social impact venture.

This foundation shapes how I approach every technical challenge with a dual lens: Is it technically sound, and does it solve a meaningful human problem? It's why I chose Georgia Tech's rigorous dual-degree path in Computer Engineering and Aerospace Engineering.

I believe the future of secure, autonomous systems — whether in aerospace or embedded computing — lies at the intersection of two threads: Cybersecurity and Computer Hardware. The Cybersecurity thread addresses and mitigates vulnerabilities; the Hardware thread designs security directly into physical infrastructure.

Beyond the code, my identity is defined by leadership and mentorship. I've guided young women aged 12–18 through Technovation and STEMin Africa, and my selection for the competitive Aspire Leaders Program (Harvard faculty-led) solidified my approach to intentional, civic-purpose leadership.
